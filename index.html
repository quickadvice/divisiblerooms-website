You have a conference room with an operable wall. It can split into two rooms, or open into one large suite.

Outlook has no way to book it as a suite. You book Room A. You have to remember to block Room B. When you slip, meetings collide.

Request a demo

DivisibleRooms is an Outlook add-in that closes this gap. Users book a named suite from the DivisibleRooms add-in, without ever leaving the Outlook compose window. The system checks availability, writes the room bookings, and protects reconfiguration time automatically.

Users never manually select or type room email addresses. The add-in adds them automatically to the invite when a suite is selected. Pick a suite. Send the invite. It works.

Exchange-native, not Exchange-synced

Direct writes to Exchange. No sync lag, no connector dependency, no extra configuration.

Works with Microsoft Teams Rooms

Suite bookings display correctly on Teams Rooms panels with no additional setup.

Pure Outlook experience

The add-in appears in compose and disappears otherwise. No portal, no new login, no interface to learn.

Multi-tenant, fully isolated

One deployed instance serves every customer, with full tenant isolation. Each organization's data, credentials, and configuration stay separate at every layer, from authentication down to the database.

Your facilities team stays informed automatically

A scheduled report shows booking activity and transition window compliance, so your team stays on top of every suite's activity, without checking in manually. See how reporting works →

Supported platforms

V1 supported: Classic Outlook for Windows, New Outlook for Windows, and Outlook Web Access (OWA).

Outlook for Mac and Outlook Mobile: planned for V2.
